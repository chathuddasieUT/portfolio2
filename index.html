Career

Research Experience

May 2021 – Present
Graduate Research Assistant
University of Tennessee, Knoxville
  • Led research on chromosome organization and intracellular dynamics in E. coli, combining imaging, genetic engineering, and computational modeling.
  • Conducted 60+ high-throughput time-lapse fluorescence microscopy experiments using Nikon Ti-E, enhancing temporal resolution from 3 minutes to 6 seconds through custom laser integration.
  • Developed deep learning–based image analysis tools (U-Net) for microscopy data.
  • Engineered 9+ bacterial strains with fluorescent labels for live-cell imaging.
  • Mentored 6+ graduate and undergraduate students in experimental techniques and data analysis.
August 2022 – May 2025
Facility User — Cleanroom Microfabrication
Oak Ridge National Laboratory · CNMS
  • Designed and fabricated microfluidic devices using e-beam lithography (JEOL 9300FS), photolithography, and RIE — achieving a 140% increase in cell capture.
  • Gained independent user access to JEOL 8100FS EBL, Suss MicroTech Aligner, Thermionics VE-240, Oxford PlasmaLab 100 RIE, and Zeiss Merlin FE-SEM.
